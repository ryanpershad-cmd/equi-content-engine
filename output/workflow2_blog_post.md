# The Case for Multi-Strategy Hedge Fund Allocations in RIA Portfolios

*A conversation between Itay and Tory — Equi*

The alternatives landscape is at an inflection point. In a recent conversation, Itay and Tory sat down to discuss what's driving the shift toward hedge fund allocations in RIA portfolios — and why the next 12 months matter more than the last decade.

## The Correlation Problem

Why traditional diversification is failing advisors.

> "Look, the macro environment is as interesting as it's been in a decade." — Itay

> "The key thing for advisors to understand is that the traditional 60/40 portfolio assumption — that stocks and bonds are negatively correlated — has fundamentally broken down." — Itay

The data reinforces this view: In 2022, the correlation between the S&P 500 and the Bloomberg Aggregate Bond Index hit 95%.. And this wasn't a one-month anomaly — the rolling 12-month correlation stayed above 60% for most of 2023..

For decades, the 60/40 portfolio was the bedrock of financial planning. But when stocks and bonds move in lockstep — as they did throughout 2022 — the entire premise of that allocation breaks down. Advisors need true diversification, and that means looking beyond traditional asset classes.

## The Access Gap

What's stopping RIAs from adding institutional alternatives.

> "It's three things, and none of them are about the investment case." — Itay

> "First, access — the best multi-strategy managers have $5 to $10 million minimums, sometimes higher." — Itay

The data reinforces this view: First, access — the best multi-strategy managers have $5 to $10 million minimums, sometimes higher.. An RIA managing $3 billion across 500 client accounts can't practically allocate that way..

The irony is that the firms best positioned to benefit from alternatives are often the least equipped to access them. Independent RIAs managing $2B to $10B have sophisticated clients with institutional expectations, but they lack the institutional infrastructure to deliver.

## Industry Momentum

Smart money is moving to alternatives — the data is clear.

> "And on the portfolio construction side, we're not building a generic multi-strategy allocation." — Itay

> "We're constructing what we call a "barbell approach" — combining high-conviction, concentrated alpha generators with lower-volatility, steady-return strategies." — Itay

The data reinforces this view: Hedge fund industry net inflows were $21 billion in Q1 2025 alone.. Bridgewater's research arm published a paper showing that a 20% alternatives allocation improved risk-adjusted returns by 150 basis points annualized over a 15-year backtest..

The institutional community has been increasing alternatives allocations for years. What's new is that this consensus is now reaching the independent advisor channel — and the infrastructure is finally catching up to enable it.
